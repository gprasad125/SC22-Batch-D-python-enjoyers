Milk Quality Predictor

Has your milk gone bad? Watch how this machine learning algorithm figures it out using data.

Find Out More

Why did we choose this topic?

Well, drinking spoiled milk can lead to food poisoning, with the most common symptoms being abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea. Thus, we wanted to build an AI model that can predict the quality of milk (high, medium, or low) to prevent food poisoning.

Let's get Started!

Variables

Turbidity

Turbidity measures the clarity of a liquid

Odor

The smell of spoiled milk is caused by the growth of lactic acid producing organisms

Taste

Spoiled milk tastes tart, while higher grade milk tastes sweet and bland

Fat

High grade whole milk is 3.25% fat

pH

The ideal pH of milk lies between 6 and 7

Temperature

The ideal temperature for milk is under 46⁰ C or 114.8⁰ F

Plots

Turbidity measures the clarity of a liquid. Milk with higher turbidity (represented by a 1) is more opaque than milk with a lower turbidity (0). Lower grade milk tends to have higher turbidity.

The rancid odor (0) of spoiled milk is caused by the growth of lactic acid producing organisms in the milk. High grade milk only has fewer samples that have a bad odor.

Spoiled milk tastes tart (0), while higher grade milk tastes sweet and bland (1). High grade milk is usually more bland, but can taste tart even though it isn't spoiled.

Since whole milk was used for this data set, the ideal fat content was 3.25% (1); however as the quality degrades the fat percentage drops (0).

The pH is a logarithmic scale that shows acidic levels, with 7 being neutral, lower than 7 being more acidic, and higher more alkaline. High grade and medium grade milk is between 6-7 pH and under 46⁰C, indicating that the ideal pH lies between 6 and 7 with an ideal temperature under 46⁰C.

This graph shows the variation in pH through our entire dataset. Almost ¼ of the milk data is 6.8 pH, and 65.45% of the data is between 6.5 - 6.8 pH.
